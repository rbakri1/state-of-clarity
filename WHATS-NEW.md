## ðŸŽ“ Key Learnings from Building This

### **1. First-Principles Coherence Is Measurable**
The LLM-as-judge prompt in `ARCHITECTURE.md:268-320` evaluates:
- Foundation Clarity: Are axioms stated?
- Logical Chain Integrity: Do claims build from foundations?
- Internal Consistency: Do sections contradict?
- Assumption Transparency: Are hidden assumptions exposed?

This is now the **highest-weighted factor (25%)** - higher than source diversity or recency.

### **2. Foundational vs. Applied Topics Need Different Treatment**
- **Foundational:** Historical Summary + Principles + longer narrative
- **Applied:** Recent empirical data + case studies + shorter narrative

Your template accommodates both by making sections optional.

### **3. Points of Contention Prevent Concept-Smuggling**
Example from "What is a State?" brief:
> **Social Contract:** "Whether any such 'contract' ever existed historically; whether consent is genuine or coerced; what happens when the contract is broken."

This forces intellectual honesty - can't just define terms favorably.

### **4. Grift Analysis Is Differentiating**
No other platform I've seen requires analyzing **opportunities for corruption** in every policy. This is:
- Cynical but realistic
- Forces second-order thinking
- Prevents naive technocracy
